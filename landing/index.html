EXHIBIT 01 · THE TRUST DEFICIT

90% adoption.
24% trust.

DORA measured the gap in 2025: teams adopted AI on infrastructure far faster than they learned to trust it. On open benchmarks, state-of-the-art agents resolve roughly one in ten realistic SRE scenarios — while every vendor accuracy number you have read is self-reported. The failure mode is always the same: a single megaprompt guesses a “probable” root cause and calls it a day.

opsbench bans the word.

CONFIRMED FALSIFIED NEED-MORE-EVIDENCE INCONCLUSIVE

The only verdicts an opsbench agent may render. “Probable” is a forbidden word — rejected by a PostToolUse hook at write time.

EXHIBIT 02 · CEDAR-GATED MUTATIONS

No agent touches production on its own judgment.

Every tool call passes a deterministic, default-deny policy gate that lives outside the model’s reasoning loop. Per-agent least-privilege allowlists decide what each specialist may touch; loop governors cap iteration; hooks intercept before and after every call. The model’s confidence is never the enforcement mechanism.

tools.cedar

Per-agent tool allowlists. An evidence collector can read a cluster; it cannot write to one. Deny by default.

governors.cedar

Loop caps and iteration discipline. Investigations converge or escalate — they never spiral.

4 hooks

PreToolUse, PostToolUse, SessionStart, SubagentStop — the tripwires around every action, including denials.

EXHIBIT 03 · CHAIN OF CUSTODY

Sealed the moment it’s collected.

Evidence is gathered in parallel across seven layers — application, node, network, storage, control plane, observability, and history — then sealed with SHA-256 manifests and entered into a custody log, per NIST SP 800-86. Hypothesis agents investigate verdict-blind: one subagent per hypothesis, none aware of the others’ conclusions, every claim pinned to a file:timestamp citation.

“A claim without a citation is a rumor.”

— the opsbench constitution

— docs/concepts/tone-and-constitution.md · NIST SP 800-86 · ISO/IEC 27037

EXHIBIT 04 · 33 SPECIALISTS, ONE DISCIPLINE

Not one megaprompt. A disciplined team.

An incident commander orchestrates the outer DAG. Collectors sweep each layer in parallel. A cataloger and a witness seal what they find. Hypothesis specialists investigate independently, a forensic synthesizer writes the narrative, and an enforcement pass — schema validator, citation checker, redaction checker, tone reviewer — blocks anything that doesn’t meet the constitution. If the verdict isn’t CONFIRMED, the investigation loops. Up to five rounds.

EXHIBIT 05 · HUMAN-GATED

No mutation without a human.

Quarantine comes first — clients scaled to zero, default-deny network policy — so the investigation can’t trample its own evidence. From there, nothing mutates without explicit human approval at each round boundary and Cedar authorization at each tool call. Recovery runs through the same approval queue as everything else. When the case closes, the team authors the full post-incident suite: a NIST 800-61 incident report, a 5-Whys RCA, CAPA mitigations, and a NIST 800-86 investigation report.
