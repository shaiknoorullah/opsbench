Every incident review has heard it: *"the probable root cause was the deployment."*
The word does a lot of quiet damage. It closes the investigation while sounding
like a conclusion. It lets a hypothesis graduate to history without ever being
tested. Six months later the same incident happens again, because "probable"
was never CONFIRMED — it was just tired people agreeing to stop looking.

opsbench takes a blunt position: **the word is banned.** A forensic artifact
may render exactly four verdicts:

- `CONFIRMED` — the evidence proves it, with citations
- `FALSIFIED` — the evidence disproves it, with citations
- `NEED-MORE-EVIDENCE` — the honest middle state
- `INCONCLUSIVE` — the evidence cannot decide either way

There is no fifth verdict. There is especially no verdict that means
"we ran out of patience but want to sound finished."

## Enforcement, not encouragement

A style guide nobody enforces is a suggestion. The constitution is enforced in
three layers, and the interesting one is the last:

1. **Skill-level instruction** — every skill tells its agents the rules.
2. **The tone-reviewer subagent** — reviews every artifact before it is
   written, revising against the constitution's principles.
3. **A PostToolUse hook** — greps every `Write` call for forbidden words and
   rejects the call outright if one appears.

The hook is the part that matters. Agents, like people, drift under pressure —
late in a long investigation, a model will reach for "probably" for the same
reason an exhausted engineer does. The hook does not negotiate. The write
fails, the agent rephrases, and the artifact that lands on disk either commits
to a verdict or admits it does not have one.

## What it changes

The practical effect is that investigations loop. If round three ends at
`NEED-MORE-EVIDENCE`, the commander sends collectors back out — up to five
rounds — instead of shipping a maybe. Post-incident reports stop containing
soft conclusions, because soft conclusions are unwritable.

> A claim without a citation is a rumor.

The ban is one sentence in a constitution file. It is also the entire
difference between an agent that investigates and an agent that guesses with
confidence formatting.
